Copyright Takedown Policy (DMCA)

Effective Date: March 15, 2026
Last Updated: March 15, 2026

RadioV2 ("the App") respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), 17 U.S.C. Section 512. This policy explains how to submit a takedown notice and what happens next.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. DESIGNATED AGENT
------------------------------------------------------------------------

All Notices of Infringement must be sent to:

  DMCA Agent: [Your Name]
  Email:      [your-email@example.com]
  Subject:    DMCA Takedown Notice - [Station Name]

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHAT TO INCLUDE IN A TAKEDOWN NOTICE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

To be valid, your notice must include ALL of the following:

  1. Identification of the copyrighted work
     Describe the work you claim is being infringed (e.g., "The broadcast
     stream for [Station Name], owned by [Rights Holder]").

  2. Identification of the infringing content
     Provide the exact Station Name or Stream URL as it appears in the App
     so we can locate and remove it promptly.

  3. Your contact information
     Full legal name, mailing address, phone number, and email address.

  4. Good Faith Statement (copy verbatim):
     "I have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner
     complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent,
     or the law."

  5. Accuracy Statement (copy verbatim):
     "The information in this notification is accurate, and under penalty
     of perjury, I am authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an
     exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."

  6. Your physical or electronic signature.

Notices missing any of the above may be considered deficient and may not
be acted upon until corrected.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. OUR RESPONSE - EXPEDITIOUS REMOVAL
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Upon receipt of a valid, complete Notice of Infringement, we will:

  - Review the notice within 5 business days.
  - Remove or disable access to the identified station/stream as quickly
    as reasonably possible.
  - Notify the submitting party (where contact information is available)
    that their content has been removed.

We act in good faith and aim to resolve all valid notices promptly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. COUNTER-NOTICE PROCEDURE
------------------------------------------------------------------------

If your station or stream was removed and you believe the removal was in
error (e.g., you are the rights holder or have authorization to stream),
you may submit a Counter-Notice to the Designated Agent listed above.

A valid Counter-Notice must include:

  1. Identification of the removed content (station name and/or URL).

  2. Your contact information (full legal name, address, phone, email).

  3. Good Faith Statement (copy verbatim):
     "I have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled
     as a result of mistake or misidentification of the material to be
     removed or disabled."

  4. Consent to Jurisdiction: a statement that you consent to the
     jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for your judicial district
     (or any judicial district if outside the United States).

  5. Your physical or electronic signature.

Upon receipt of a valid Counter-Notice, we may restore the content within
10-14 business days unless the original complainant notifies us that they
have filed a court action to restrain the alleged infringement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. REPEAT INFRINGER POLICY
------------------------------------------------------------------------

RadioV2 maintains a strict Repeat Infringer Policy:

  - Any station, stream source, or user account that is the subject of
    three or more valid takedown notices will be permanently removed from
    the App.
  - Such sources will be added to a block list and will not be re-added.
  - We reserve the right to act sooner for egregious or bad-faith
    infringement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. ABUSE OF THE TAKEDOWN PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

Submitting a knowingly false or misleading takedown notice is a violation
of 17 U.S.C. Section 512(f) and may expose the claimant to liability for
damages, including legal fees.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

rebroadcast audio content. All streams are provided by third-party radio
stations and accessed directly by users. We are not responsible for the
content of third-party streams.
